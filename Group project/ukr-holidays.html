Holidays in Ukraine

Independence Day in Ukraine is a significant national holiday celebrated on August 24. This day symbolizes Ukraine's acquisition of sovereignty after years of struggle for independence. Independence Day was officially proclaimed in 1991 following the dissolution of the Soviet Union.

The celebration begins with the solemn raising of the Ukrainian flag, the singing of the anthem, and various festive events throughout the country. In Kyiv, on the main square, Maidan Nezalezhnosti, parades, concerts, as well as various cultural and sports activities take place.

This day also serves as a time to reflect on Ukraine's historical roots, emphasizing its cultural heritage and heroic fight for freedom. National symbols, decorations in the colors of the flag, and traditional Ukrainian attire can be seen on the streets.

Defender of Ukraine Day is observed on October 14 and serves as a significant national holiday dedicated to commemorating and acknowledging the heroism of those who defend the sovereignty and independence of Ukraine. This day was established in 2014 during a period of active events in the eastern part of the country and the annexation of Crimea by Russia.

Defender of Ukraine Day is dedicated to honoring the memory of defenders, including servicemen, police officers, and all those who have devoted their lives to serving and protecting their homeland. On this day, solemn events, parades, and ceremonies for laying flowers at military memorials take place.

The celebration of Defender of Ukraine Day also involves expressions of gratitude towards the military, veterans, and their families. Society expresses respect and appreciation for those who serve in the army and police, as well as those who actively participated in defending the country during challenging periods of its history.

Maslenitsa is a traditional Slavic holiday celebrated during the week before Great Lent, preceding the Orthodox Great Fast. The celebration of Maslenitsa typically falls in February or March, depending on the calendar.

The main feature of Maslenitsa is the abundant feasting on pancakes. Pancakes symbolize the sun and the arrival of spring, and their round shape is associated with the sun disc. During this period, many families prepare pancakes of various kinds: thin, thick, with various fillings and additives.

An important part of Maslenitsa celebrations is various games, folk festivities, and entertainment. Numerous folk festivities and Maslenitsa fairs are held during this time, where one can buy various craft items and treats.

A significant conclusion to Maslenitsa is the tradition of burning the effigy, also known as "Maslenitsa" or "Wide Maslenitsa." This ritual symbolizes bidding farewell to winter and welcoming spring.
